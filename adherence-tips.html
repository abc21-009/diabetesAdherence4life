How to Stick to Your Medication Routine

Set Reminders: Use phone alarms or medication reminder apps.

Organize Your Medication: A pill organizer can help track doses.

Link to Daily Habits: Take medication with meals or while brushing your teeth.

Talk to Your Doctor: If you experience side effects, don’t stop—seek guidance instead.

Understanding Your Medications

Oral Medications

  • Biguanides: Reduce liver glucose production and improve insulin sensitivity (e.g., Metformin).
  • Sulfonylureas: Stimulate insulin secretion (e.g., Glibenclamide, Glipizide).
  • SGLT2 Inhibitors: Promote glucose excretion via urine (e.g., Empagliflozin, Canagliflozin).
  • DPP-4 Inhibitors: Enhance insulin secretion and suppress glucagon production (e.g., Sitagliptin, Linagliptin).

    • Injectable Medications

  • GLP-1 Receptor Agonists: Improve insulin release and slow digestion (e.g., Liraglutide, Semaglutide).
  • Insulin: Essential for Type 1 and sometimes Type 2 diabetes (e.g., Actrapid, Humulin R, Lantus).
